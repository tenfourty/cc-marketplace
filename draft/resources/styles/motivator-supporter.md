## Overview

Short, in-thread replies to publicly acknowledge effort, celebrate wins, and boost team morale. This is a crucial tool for positive reinforcement. Almost exclusively used in threads replying to announcements, demos, project completions, or individual achievements.

## Key Characteristics

- **Context:** Thread replies, not standalone messages
- **Brevity and impact:** Short, sincere, to the point
- **Tone:** Genuinely enthusiastic, appreciative, and supportive
- **Emoji-driven:** Relies on emojis (:tada: :clap: :rocket: :muscle:) to convey positive emotion
- **Specific praise:** Always say WHAT was great, not just "great job"
- **Purpose:** Amplify success and ensure the team feels seen and valued by leadership

## Structure Template

Short (1 line):
```
[Specific praise], [thanks/acknowledgement]! [emoji]
```

Medium (2-3 lines):
```
[What impressed you]. [Why it matters or what it enables]. [Thanks + emoji]
```

> That was impressively fast — thanks for jumping on this! :muscle:

### Example B: Praising a Demo

> Really slick demo — the UX improvements are night and day. Great work! :tada:

### Example C: Appreciating Thorough Analysis

> This is exactly the level of detail we needed. The comparison table alone saves everyone a ton of time — thank you!

### Example D: Enthusiasm for a Roadmap

> Love the ambition here. The phased rollout makes a lot of sense — thanks for the visibility on the plan!

**Pattern across all examples:** Each one names the SPECIFIC thing being praised. None are generic "good job" messages.

## Common Mistakes

- Generic praise with no specifics ("Well done!")
- Over-doing the emojis (one or two is enough)
- Making it about yourself instead of them
- Being so brief it feels dismissive
- Writing a paragraph when a sentence would have more impact

## PCM Variations

- **Thinker:** Acknowledge the quality of their thinking or analysis specifically
- **Persister:** Connect their work to team values or mission impact
- **Harmoniser:** Emphasise the personal effort and care they put in
- **Rebel:** Keep energy high, maybe add a fun comment
- **Promoter:** Acknowledge speed or decisive action
