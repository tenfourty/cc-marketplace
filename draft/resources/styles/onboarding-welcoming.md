## Overview

For welcoming new team members and helping them feel valued from day one. Warm, enthusiastic, and community-oriented. These messages set the tone for someone's entire experience.

## Key Characteristics

- **Enthusiastic tone:** Genuine excitement about the person joining.
- **Public welcome:** Announced in relevant channels to make the person visible and valued.
- **Call to action:** Encourage existing team members to welcome and help the new person.
- **Context provided:** Sometimes include role or background to help people connect.
- **Anticipation language:** "We've been eagerly waiting" or "highly anticipated" to make them feel wanted.

## Structure Template

Standard welcome:
```
[Channel], Welcome, [Name], [their role], who starts [timing]!

Please join me in giving a warm welcome to <@Name>, who joins us today as [role].

[1-2 sentences of genuine enthusiasm + what they're joining]

[Call to action for existing team — say hello, help them settle in]
```

Short welcome (multiple people or reply):
```
Welcome [Name(s)]! [Enthusiasm]. Great to have you onboard!
```

## Illustrative Examples

> **Note:** These are generic templates. Your real examples (loaded from `memory/draft/styles/onboarding-welcoming.md` if available) will be used as the primary voice reference when drafting.

### Example A: New Team Member

> #engineering, :wave: Please join me in welcoming <@Name>, who joins us today as a Backend Engineer on the Platform team!
>
> We've been looking forward to having you on board. If you're around this week, do say hello and help them find their feet — first weeks are always a whirlwind!

**Why it works:** Public, enthusiastic, specific about role and team. Asks others to take action. Warm without being over the top.

### Example B: Multiple New Joiners

> #general, Big welcome to our three new starters this week! We've been looking forward to your arrivals — great to have you all here!

### Example C: Welcome Back from Leave

> Great to have you back! Honestly felt like you never left — the team kept your momentum going brilliantly.

**Pattern across examples:** Each one is personal and specific. Even short messages convey genuine warmth. Longer forms add a practical call to action (say hello, help settle in).

## Common Mistakes

- Being so brief it feels like an afterthought
- Forgetting to tag the person (they should be notified!)
- Not posting in the right channel (public visibility matters)
- Generic template that could be for anyone
- Over-the-top enthusiasm that feels performative

## PCM Variations

- **Thinker:** Include a brief mention of what they'll be working on
- **Harmoniser:** Extra warmth, mention the team is excited to meet them
- **Rebel:** Add a fun or light-hearted comment
- **General:** Default template works — warm, public, action-oriented
