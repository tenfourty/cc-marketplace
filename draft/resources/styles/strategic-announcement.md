## Overview

Long-form, structured messages sent to a broad audience (company-wide channels, all-hands). The goal is to share high-level strategy, announce organisational changes, reflect on the past period, and motivate the team. These are the "big" messages — the ones people remember.

## Key Characteristics

- **Catchy title:** Start with a clear, branded title with emojis
- **Personal opening:** Warm greeting that connects with the reader
- **Structured body:** Logical sections with bolded headings, often preceded by relevant emojis
- **Bullet points** and nested lists for clarity
- **Bold key phrases** to emphasise important points
- **Transparent & vulnerable:** Share not just the "what" but the "why." Be open about challenges.
- **Motivational closing:** End on a high note with forward-looking energy

## Structure Template

```
[Channel tag], [Emoji] [Title]

[Personal opening — 1-2 sentences connecting with the reader]

[Section 1 heading — bolded, emoji prefix]
• Key point (bolded phrase + context)
• Key point
  ◦ Sub-point with detail

[Section 2 heading]
• ...

[Motivational closing — 1-2 sentences]

:question: Questions? Drop them in the thread :thread:

[Optional: link to full details]
```

### Example A: Quarterly Roadmap Update

> #product, :compass: Q3 Roadmap — Where We're Heading
>
> Hi folks, I wanted to share the product direction for the next quarter and the thinking behind it.
>
> **:dart: Focus area 1: Self-serve onboarding**
> We're investing heavily here because 40% of trial users drop off before seeing value. <@Name> is leading the redesign.
>
> **:shield: Focus area 2: Compliance automation**
> Several enterprise prospects have asked for this. We're pulling it forward from Q4.
>
> **What we're NOT doing this quarter:**
> We're pausing the mobile app work. I know that's disappointing for some, but we need to concentrate our efforts.
>
> :page_facing_up: <link|Full roadmap in Notion>
> :question: Questions? Drop them in the thread :thread:

**Why it works:** Clear structure with emoji headings. Explains the "why" behind each priority. Transparent about trade-offs (what we're NOT doing). Links to detail without repeating it.

### Example B: New Technology Adoption

> #engineering, Hi folks,
>
> Quick update on where we are with the migration to a new CI/CD pipeline. We've been running it in parallel on two teams for the past month and the results are promising — build times are down 35%.
>
> * We're rolling it out to all teams starting next Monday
> * <@Name> is the point of contact if you hit issues during the switch
>
> The main limitation right now is that the caching layer isn't fully optimised yet. However, the goal is to have that sorted by end of month.
>
> If you have concerns or questions, drop them in the thread.

**Why it works:** Data-driven ("down 35%"). Personal ownership. Transparent about limitations. Named point of contact for accountability.

## Common Mistakes

- Starting with boring detail instead of the exciting news
- Burying the most important change in paragraph 3
- Forgetting the motivational close
- Not providing a questions/thread prompt
- Making it too formal — this should feel like a person talking, not a press release

## PCM Variations

- **Thinker audience:** Add more detail on the reasoning and data behind decisions
- **Persister audience:** Emphasise how changes align with company values and mission
- **Harmoniser audience:** Lead with how this affects people, acknowledge emotions
- **General audience:** Default template works well — it naturally balances structure and warmth
