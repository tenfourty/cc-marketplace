## Overview
Stephen Covey's framework is built on the distinction between the character ethic (effectiveness grounded in principles and integrity) and the personality ethic (effectiveness as technique and image). His 7 Habits move from private victories (personal mastery) to public victories (relationships and collaboration) to renewal. The Circle of Influence and time management quadrants are among the most widely useful diagnostic tools in coaching.

## When to Use
- Person is reactive — constantly responding to others' urgencies and losing sight of what matters to them
- Person is spending time on what's urgent at the expense of what's important (quadrant analysis)
- Person's stated priorities and actual time allocation are misaligned
- Person needs to define their personal mission — what they stand for, not just what they do
- Person's relationships have trust deficits — the emotional bank account concept applies directly
- Person is winning arguments and losing relationships (Habit 5: seek first to understand)

## When NOT to Use
- Person needs acute state management or motivation (Robbins, Burchard first)
- Behavioral mechanics issues — this framework operates at the values/principle level, not the habit-design level
- When speed is essential — Covey's framework rewards deep reflection and is not a quick intervention
- Person is in crisis — principle-centered thinking requires stability to apply

## Core Philosophy
Effectiveness is about both the production of desired results AND the preservation of the capacity to produce (P/PC Balance). Most people and organizations sacrifice the goose (capacity) for the golden eggs (short-term results). Covey grounds everything in principles — natural laws that govern human effectiveness — rather than techniques. The habits move from dependence → independence → interdependence: the highest level of maturity.

**Character before technique:** The 7 Habits will not work as techniques grafted onto a character that contradicts them. They require genuine internalization of the underlying principles.

## Key Methods

**Circle of Concern vs Circle of Influence (Habit 1: Be Proactive)**
- Circle of Concern: everything you care about (weather, economy, others' behavior)
- Circle of Influence: what you can actually affect
- Reactive people focus energy on the Circle of Concern — and it shrinks their Circle of Influence
- Proactive people focus energy on the Circle of Influence — and it expands
- Diagnostic: "Where is this person's energy directed right now? Concern or Influence?"
- Language tells you: "If only..." / "They..." / "There's nothing I can do" = reactive. "I will..." / "I can..." / "I choose..." = proactive.

**Begin with the End in Mind (Habit 2)**
- All things are created twice: first in the mind, then in reality
- Personal Mission Statement: your constitution — what you stand for, what kind of person you want to be, what contributions you want to make
- Use the "funeral test": at the end of your life, what would you want your closest relationships to say about you?
- Leadership is doing the right things; management is doing things right. Habit 2 is leadership.

**Put First Things First (Habit 3) — Time Management Matrix**
- Four quadrants based on Urgency (now vs not now) and Importance (matters vs doesn't matter):
  - **Q1: Urgent + Important** — crises, deadlines, emergencies (necessary but not where you want to live)
  - **Q2: Not Urgent + Important** — relationships, planning, skill development, renewal (the effectiveness quadrant)
  - **Q3: Urgent + Not Important** — interruptions, most meetings, others' urgencies (main time thief)
  - **Q4: Not Urgent + Not Important** — trivia, time-wasters (to eliminate)
- High performers live in Q2. Most people are pulled between Q1 and Q3.
- Diagnostic: map the past week's activities into quadrants. The result is usually surprising.

**Think Win-Win (Habit 4)**
- Win-Win is not compromise — it is a mindset of abundance that genuinely seeks mutual benefit
- Win-Lose (competition) and Lose-Win (doormat) are both inferior and unsustainable
- Emotional Bank Account: every interaction with another person either deposits (kindness, kept promises, honoring dignity) or withdraws (broken promises, disrespect, ignoring)
- You can only operate from Win-Win when your emotional bank accounts with others are in credit

**Seek First to Understand, Then to Be Understood (Habit 5)**
- Empathic listening: listening to understand, not to reply
- Most people listen autobiographically: filtering what they hear through their own experience, preparing their response, projecting meaning
- Empathic listening requires setting aside your own frame and genuinely entering the other person's frame first
- Only after you have demonstrated that you understand should you offer your perspective
- "Diagnose before you prescribe" — a doctor who prescribes without diagnosing is dangerous

**Synergize (Habit 6)**
- The whole is greater than the sum of parts when relationships have high trust and people genuinely value differences
- Synergy is not compromise (1+1=1.5) — it is creative cooperation (1+1=3 or more)
- Prerequisite: Win-Win mindset + empathic communication. Without these, synergy is not accessible.

**Sharpen the Saw (Habit 7) — Renewal**
- Renewal across four dimensions, all of which must be maintained:
  - **Physical:** exercise, nutrition, rest, stress management
  - **Social/Emotional:** relationships, service, empathy, security from within
  - **Mental:** reading, learning, writing, teaching
  - **Spiritual:** values clarification, prayer/meditation, time in nature, the arts
- Neglecting any dimension erodes the capacity to practice the other habits
- This is the P in P/PC Balance — the goose that lays the golden eggs

**Emotional Bank Account**
- A metaphor for the level of trust in a relationship
- Deposits: keeping commitments, kindness, honoring dignity, clarifying expectations, apologizing genuinely, loyalty to the absent
- Withdrawals: broken commitments, discourtesy, disrespect, betrayal, threats
- When the account is in credit, communication is easy and mistakes are forgiven; when overdrawn, every interaction is defensive

## Key Questions
- "Where is your energy going — your Circle of Concern or your Circle of Influence? What's the effect?"
- "If you mapped last week's calendar into the four quadrants, what would you find? What would you want to change?"
- "What would you want the people who matter most to you to say about you at the end of your life? Are you living that way now?"
- "What does your personal mission statement say? If you don't have one, what would it say if you wrote it today?"
- "In this relationship, is your emotional bank account in credit or overdrawn? What's your most recent deposit?"
- "Are you listening to understand, or to reply? What would change if you listened to understand first?"
- "Which of the four renewal dimensions are you most neglecting? What is the cost?"
- "Are you being efficient with people and effective with things, or have you reversed that?"

## Pitfalls
- **Treating the 7 Habits as techniques:** Covey is explicit — these are principles, not tips. Habit 4 (Win-Win) without genuine character commitment becomes manipulation. Character must lead.
- **Skipping the private victories:** Habits 4-6 (public victories) require the foundation of Habits 1-3 (private victories). Don't lead with synergy when proactivity is broken.
- **Quadrant analysis without calendar change:** The exercise only has value if Q2 time gets protected. Map it, then restructure the calendar.
- **Empathic listening as technique:** Seeking first to understand only works if it is genuine. Performed empathy is worse than not trying.
- **Sharpen the Saw last, least, optional:** Most people treat Habit 7 as a nice-to-have. Covey treats it as the habit that makes all others possible.
- **Win-Win or no deal — the "no deal" part:** Win-Win only works if you're genuinely willing to walk away from deals that aren't mutual. The option of No Deal must be real.
- **Mission statement as poster:** A mission statement that lives in a drawer has no effect. It needs to be the filter for actual decisions.
