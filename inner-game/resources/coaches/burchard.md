## Overview
Brendon Burchard identified six habits — through research across thousands of high performers — that distinguish those who sustain excellence long-term from those who peak and burn out. Unlike motivational frameworks, HP6 is diagnostic: you can assess which of the six habits is your current constraint. The transition prompt and "best self" question are his most immediately actionable tools.

## When to Use
- Person wants a comprehensive, research-backed framework for sustained high performance
- Person is performing well in some areas but stalling in others — HP6 identifies the constraint
- Person is prone to burnout — the HP6 framework has specific protections against it (energy, courage)
- Person is a leader who needs to influence others without direct authority (influence habit)
- Person moves rapidly between contexts (meetings, projects, roles) and loses quality and presence in transitions

## When NOT to Use
- Person needs an acute state shift right now (Robbins is faster)
- Person needs root identity work before performance optimization is meaningful (Hardison first)
- Person is in crisis — HP6 is a growth framework, not a recovery one
- Person is just beginning personal development — HP6 assumes a baseline of self-awareness and functioning

## Core Philosophy
High performance is not the result of talent, luck, or grinding harder. It is the result of six specific, learnable habits practiced consistently. Critically, these habits must all be active — neglecting any one creates a ceiling or a crash. Burchard's research finding: the habits that distinguish long-term high performers are about clarity, energy, necessity, productivity, influence, and courage — not intelligence, charisma, or any fixed trait.

**Sustained success vs peak:** Many people achieve peaks of performance. Fewer sustain excellence without burning out. HP6 is specifically designed for the long game.

## Key Methods

**The HP6 (Six High Performance Habits)**

**HP1: Seek Clarity**
- Know who you want to be, how you want to treat others, what skills you want to develop, and what contributions you want to make
- Practice: "How can I show up as my best self in this next activity?"
- Clarity is not a one-time insight — it requires active, recurring review
- Define success for each domain of your life, not just career

**HP2: Generate Energy**
- Physical energy (sleep, movement, nutrition), emotional energy (managing negativity, relationships), mental energy (focus, recovery)
- Key practice: transition rituals between activities — release the last context, set intention for the next
- "Release, reset, reenter" between meetings and role switches
- Protect recovery time as seriously as productive time

**HP3: Raise Necessity**
- High performers feel a compelling reason to perform — internal standards, external commitments, or both
- "I must perform well because..." — if this sentence doesn't have a strong answer, performance suffers
- Sources of necessity: identity ("this is who I am"), obsession, real stakes, accountability to others
- Build in real consequences and public commitments

**HP4: Increase Productivity**
- Output, not input — time on the single most important projects in your field
- Prolific quality output (PQO): identify the output that matters most in your role and do more of it
- Master the primary skill progressions required at the next level
- Eliminate energy-draining distractions and marginal work

**HP5: Develop Influence**
- High performers think about shaping others' thinking, not just their own performance
- Teach your framework — share your mental models with the people around you
- "Challenge the challenge" — express belief in others' ability to grow
- Build relationships that extend your reach through trust and reputation

**HP6: Demonstrate Courage**
- Speak up, share your voice, take bold action even under uncertainty
- High performers feel the fear and act anyway — they've normalized discomfort
- "What would I do here if I were being bold?"
- Defend your values publicly — your team watches whether you walk the talk

**The Transition Prompt**
- Between activities, ask: "What kind of energy and presence do I want to bring to this next thing?"
- Take 60 seconds to release the previous context, set intention, recall your best self
- Prevents carryover of negativity, distraction, or rushed energy from one context to the next
- Deceptively simple; dramatically changes meeting and conversation quality

**The HP6 Self-Assessment**
- Rate yourself 1-10 on each of the six habits
- Identify the lowest-scoring habit — that is almost always the current constraint
- Focus improvement effort on the lowest habit for 90 days, then reassess

**"Best Self" Question**
- Before any important meeting, conversation, or decision: "How would my best self show up here?"
- Creates a mental model of the person you want to be in this specific context
- Can be combined with the transition prompt for maximum effect

## Key Questions
- "Which of the six habits is your current constraint — where are you scoring lowest?"
- "How would your best self show up in this next conversation/meeting/decision?"
- "What is your necessity? Why MUST you perform at a high level? What's at stake?"
- "What is the one output that matters most in your role right now — your PQO — and are you protecting time for it?"
- "Who in your life are you actively developing and encouraging? Are you teaching your frameworks?"
- "Where are you playing it safe when you know you should speak up or take a bolder position?"
- "How are you generating energy right now — physically, emotionally, mentally? Where is the leak?"
- "What would you release from the last hour before you enter this next conversation?"

## Pitfalls
- **Using HP6 as inspiration without assessment:** The framework only delivers if you honestly assess and identify your lowest habit. Don't just read the list and feel good about it.
- **Treating all six as equal priority:** They're not equal — your lowest score is your bottleneck. Concentrate there.
- **Productivity habit as "stay busy":** PQO is about quality output on what matters most, not volume. More activity is often the opposite of this habit.
- **Skipping transition rituals:** This is the easiest habit to implement and has an outsized return. If someone takes only one thing from Burchard, this is it.
- **Necessity from external fear only:** Necessity driven purely by fear of failure is unstable. Help the person find intrinsic sources (identity, craft standards, genuine contribution).
- **Courage without clarity:** Bold action without clear values and direction is noise. HP1 (Clarity) is the foundation — if it's weak, HP6 misfires.
