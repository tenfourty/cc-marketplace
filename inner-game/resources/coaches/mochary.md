## Overview
Matt Mochary coaches founders and CEOs with a methodology built on two pillars: ruthless energy management (only do what gives you energy, delegate the rest) and conscious leadership (identify whether you're operating above or below the line in any moment). His approach is intensely practical and system-oriented — every insight becomes a process.

## When to Use
- Person is overwhelmed, burnt out, or doing work that drains them
- Person is reacting from fear or anger and making poor decisions
- Person needs to redesign their calendar around their actual energy
- Person has broken agreements creating trust debt with team or relationships
- Person needs a structured leadership system, not just inspiration

## When NOT to Use
- Person needs deep identity/BEing work first (see Hardison)
- Acute emotional crisis — clear the emotion first before system-building
- Person lacks basic self-awareness about their own emotional states
- When the issue is motivation (Robbins) rather than energy/system design

## Core Philosophy
Most leaders fill their calendars with what seems urgent or important rather than what they are uniquely suited to and energized by. Mochary's insight: your energy is your leadership performance. When you operate from fear or anger (below the line), you make worse decisions and poison team culture. The system fixes are permanent only when the emotional foundation is stable.

**Conscious Leadership (Above/Below the Line):**
- Above the line: curious, open, learning, trusting, creating
- Below the line: defensive, closed, protecting, closed to feedback, winning/being right
- The line is not good/bad — it is a factual orientation. Most people are below the line most of the time.

## Key Methods

**Energy Audit**
- List all your regular activities (meetings, tasks, recurring work)
- Label each: gives energy (green) / neutral (yellow) / drains energy (red)
- Ruthlessly eliminate or delegate red and yellow items
- Redesign calendar so 80%+ of time is in green activities
- Output: a restructured role description and delegated task list

**Above/Below the Line Check**
- Ask in any moment: "Am I above or below the line right now?"
- Below-the-line indicators: defensiveness, blaming, justifying, closed to input, winning orientation
- To shift above: name it ("I notice I'm below the line"), breathe, get curious
- Do not make important decisions below the line

**Fear/Anger/Sadness Clearing**
- Mochary follows a specific protocol: identify the emotion, find where it lives in the body, speak it out loud with a witness (coach or trusted colleague)
- Say the feeling fully: "I feel [anger/fear/sadness] about..."
- Repeat until the emotion discharges — the body releases it
- Used before important conversations, decisions, or feedback sessions
- Prevents emotion from leaking into behavior

**Impeccable Agreements**
- An agreement is impeccable when: clearly made, with a specific deadline, by someone who genuinely intends to keep it
- When an agreement will be broken, notify immediately — renegotiate proactively
- Track agreements explicitly (written, with owner and date)
- Broken agreements = trust debt; accumulated trust debt = org dysfunction

**Top Goal / Weekly Review**
- Identify the single most important goal each week
- Allocate minimum 2 uninterrupted hours per week to it (not meeting time)
- Weekly review: Did I advance my top goal? What agreements did I break? What do I need to clear?

**Feedback System**
- Real-time feedback culture: give feedback within 24 hours
- Format: "When you did X, I felt Y, and it impacted Z. In the future, I'd prefer..."
- No feedback sandwiches — direct and kind simultaneously

## Key Questions
- "Is this activity giving you energy or draining it? What would need to be true to remove it from your plate?"
- "Are you above or below the line right now? What is the indicator?"
- "What are you afraid of in this situation? Say it out loud."
- "What agreements have you broken this week? To whom do you owe a renegotiation?"
- "If you could only work on one thing this week, what would move the needle most?"
- "Who is uniquely able to do this? If it's not you, who?"
- "What emotion are you bringing into this room that isn't about this situation?"

## Pitfalls
- **Energy audit without delegation infrastructure:** If you audit but can't delegate, nothing changes. Build delegation capacity first.
- **Emotional clearing as performance:** The protocol only works if the emotion is genuinely expressed, not described. Describing feelings is not the same as feeling them.
- **Below-the-line denial:** "I'm not below the line, this person is actually wrong" is classic below-the-line reasoning. The defensiveness is the tell.
- **Impeccable agreements applied only downward:** Leaders must hold themselves to the same standard or the system breeds resentment.
- **Skipping weekly review:** The system degrades without the review loop. It is not optional.
- **Using top-goal time for meetings:** Protect the deep-work block. This is its entire point.
