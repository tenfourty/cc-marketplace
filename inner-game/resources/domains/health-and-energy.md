## Overview
Health and energy is the foundation domain — without physical vitality, performance in every other life area degrades. This domain covers not just absence of illness but the presence of genuine energy: the capacity to show up fully for work, relationships, and what matters. Energy is the resource that underlies all other resources.

## Key Dimensions
- **Sleep quality** — duration, consistency, wake feeling rested
- **Physical activity** — frequency, intensity, variety, enjoyment
- **Nutrition** — food quality, eating patterns, relationship with food
- **Stress management** — recovery practices, stress load vs. capacity
- **Energy rhythms** — peak hours, ultradian rhythms, strategic scheduling
- **Body relationship** — listening to body signals, self-compassion around health

## Assessment Questions
- When did you last wake up feeling genuinely rested and energised? What was different then?
- On a typical weekday, when is your energy highest? Lowest? How does your schedule reflect this?
- What is your body asking for right now that you've been ignoring?
- If your body could speak, what complaint would it lead with?
- What does "taking care of yourself" mean to you — and is that definition working?
- How much of your stress is genuinely unavoidable vs. self-generated through habits and choices?
- What is the single health behaviour whose absence costs you the most?

## Common Patterns
- **Ignoring body signals** — treating fatigue, pain, and tension as inconveniences rather than information. The body always wins eventually.
- **All-or-nothing exercise** — either training for an event or completely sedentary. No sustainable middle ground.
- **Using food for emotional regulation** — eating for comfort, reward, or numbing rather than fuel. Coaching this requires gentleness.
- **Sleep deprivation as identity** — "I only need five hours" as a badge of productivity. Often correlates with below-average cognitive performance.
- **Boom-bust energy cycles** — grinding to exhaustion, then collapsing. Treating recovery as failure rather than strategy.
- **Treating health as future-self's problem** — "I'll sort this out when work calms down."

## Quick Wins
- Set a non-negotiable sleep window — same bedtime, seven nights in a row
- Add one 20-minute walk per day (after lunch is ideal for energy and blood sugar)
- Drink water before coffee in the morning
- Identify your one highest-energy hour and protect it from meetings for two weeks
- Do a five-minute body scan at end of each workday: what is tension telling you?

## Deep Work
- **Energy audit** — track energy levels hourly for five days (1-10 scale, brief note on cause). Map to activities, food, sleep, and social contact. Identify patterns.
- **Identify energy anchors and drains** — two lists: what reliably gives energy, what reliably takes it. Look for misalignment with how time is allocated.
- **Design a minimum viable health protocol** — what is the smallest set of habits that keeps you functional? Build this floor before building the ceiling.
- **Read:** *Why We Sleep* by Matthew Walker (sleep science); *The Body Keeps the Score* by Bessel van der Kolk (stress and body); *Atomic Habits* by James Clear (habit formation)

## Cross-Domain Connections
- **Inner life** — emotional stress and unprocessed feelings manifest as physical symptoms. Anxiety disrupts sleep; depression reduces activity. See `inner-life.md`.
- **Career and purpose** — misaligned work is a chronic stressor. Burnout is often a career alignment problem wearing a health costume. See `career-and-purpose.md`.
- **Fun and adventure** — play and physical joy (sport, dance, nature) are health behaviours, not luxuries. See `fun-and-adventure.md`.
- **Relationships** — social isolation is as damaging to health as smoking. Connection is physiologically restorative. See `relationships.md`.

## Pitfalls
- **Medicalising what is a lifestyle question** — resist the urge to defer everything to "see a doctor." Most energy problems are behavioural.
- **Optimisation before foundations** — don't coach biohacking protocols when the person sleeps five hours and never moves. Fix basics first.
- **Shaming around health failures** — this domain carries significant guilt and self-judgment. Lead with curiosity, not improvement pressure.
- **Ignoring mental health as health** — anxiety, depression, and chronic stress are health issues, not character issues. Don't coach past them.
