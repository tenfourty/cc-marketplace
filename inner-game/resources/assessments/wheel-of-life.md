## Overview
The Wheel of Life is a foundational coaching tool that maps satisfaction across seven life domains on a 1–10 scale. The visual gap between domains makes imbalances immediately obvious. It works best as a recurring benchmark — run it every 4–12 weeks to track trend lines, not just snapshots.

## When to Use
- Opening a new coaching engagement to establish a baseline
- User says something feels off but can't identify which area
- After a major life transition (new job, relationship change, move)
- Quarterly or end-of-year review rhythm
- User has been focused on one area at the expense of others

## When NOT to Use
- User is in acute crisis — move to stabilization first, not assessment
- User just completed a Wheel of Life session in the last 4 weeks (wait for meaningful time to pass)
- User wants to go deep on one domain — use a domain-specific assessment instead
- User is resistant to scoring; adapt to a qualitative conversation instead

## The Assessment

Ask the user to rate their current satisfaction (not importance, not potential) in each domain from 1 to 10. 1 = very dissatisfied, 10 = fully satisfied and thriving. Remind them: these are gut-feel scores, not analytical calculations.

**Domain 1: Health & Energy**
- How would you rate your physical health right now?
- Do you have the energy to do what matters to you?
- Are you sleeping well and recovering adequately?
- Are you moving your body in ways that feel good?
Score: ___/10

**Domain 2: Relationships**
- How satisfied are you with your closest relationships (partner, family, close friends)?
- Do you feel genuinely connected to the people who matter most?
- Are your relationships reciprocal and nourishing?
- Do you have enough depth in your social world?
Score: ___/10

**Domain 3: Career & Purpose**
- How meaningful and engaging is your work?
- Are you using your strongest skills?
- Does your work align with what you care about?
- Do you feel a sense of forward momentum professionally?
Score: ___/10

**Domain 4: Finances**
- Are you financially stable and free from urgent money stress?
- Are you making progress toward your financial goals?
- Do you have enough financial freedom to make choices that matter to you?
Score: ___/10

**Domain 5: Growth & Learning**
- Are you actively learning and developing?
- Do you feel intellectually stimulated?
- Are you becoming more capable and knowledgeable over time?
Score: ___/10

**Domain 6: Fun & Adventure**
- Are you having enough fun?
- Do you have hobbies, play, or experiences that light you up?
- Is there spontaneity or adventure in your life?
Score: ___/10

**Domain 7: Inner Life**
- Do you feel a sense of peace and groundedness?
- Are you living in alignment with your values?
- Do you have a spiritual, meditative, or reflective practice that sustains you?
- Do you feel a sense of meaning beyond day-to-day tasks?
Score: ___/10

**Total:** ___/70

## Interpretation Guide

**By score:**
- 8–10: Thriving in this domain. Ask: what's working that you want to protect?
- 6–7: Functional but with noticeable gaps. Ask: what's the one thing that would move this?
- 4–5: Significant dissatisfaction. This domain likely has a cost — explore spillover effects.
- 1–3: Crisis or neglect. Needs attention before other domains can improve.

**By pattern:**
- Uniformly high (all 6+): Explore whether scores reflect reality or optimism bias. Ask where they'd score a 10 and what's stopping them.
- Uniformly low (all below 5): Check for depression, burnout, or overwhelming life circumstances. Go slow.
- Sharp gap between domains: The low-scoring domains often have spillover costs on the high-scoring ones. Name that explicitly.
- One outlier low: That domain is usually the lever. Focus there.

**Trend reading (when used repeatedly):**
- A rising score in one domain with a falling score in another often indicates a trade-off being made. Name it.
- Consistent flatlines in a domain suggest habituation or avoidance. Worth probing.
- Any domain dropping 2+ points since last session: treat as a priority signal.

## Follow-Up Questions
- Looking at this wheel, if it were literally a wheel — would it roll smoothly, or would it be bumpy? What does that tell you?
- Which score surprises you most?
- Which low score has the biggest ripple effect on the others?
- If you could only move one score by 2 points in the next 90 days, which would create the most change in your overall life?
- What would a 10 in [lowest domain] actually look like? What's the gap?
- Are any of these scores where they are by choice? Is that a conscious trade-off?

## Pitfalls
- Don't let users score on importance instead of satisfaction — redirect if they start rationalizing
- Avoid jumping to problem-solving on low scores immediately; hold the whole picture first
- Don't treat the wheel as a to-do list — the goal is awareness and choice, not optimizing every domain to 10
- Watch for deflated scores due to temporary circumstances (bad week) — ask about the last 4 weeks, not just today
- If a user scores everything 7+, gently probe for the domain they left off or are avoiding
