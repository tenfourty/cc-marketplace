## Overview
PERMA is Martin Seligman's evidence-based model of wellbeing from positive psychology. It identifies five measurable pillars of flourishing: Positive Emotion, Engagement, Relationships, Meaning, and Achievement. Unlike the Wheel of Life's broad life-domain view, PERMA goes deeper on the inner experience of wellbeing — useful when someone looks fine on the outside but doesn't feel good on the inside.

## When to Use
- User reports feeling flat, empty, or unfulfilled despite outward success
- User is questioning whether they are actually happy or just busy
- Follow-up after a Wheel of Life when wellbeing domains score low
- User recovering from burnout and rebuilding a sustainable life
- User wants a more research-grounded lens than general life satisfaction

## When NOT to Use
- User needs domain-specific help (career, relationships, finances) — Wheel of Life is broader and more useful first
- User is in acute emotional distress — PERMA is a flourishing model, not a crisis tool
- User is highly skeptical of frameworks — keep it conversational instead
- Already completed PERMA in the last 6 weeks without significant life change

## The Assessment

Ask the user to rate each question from 0 to 10. 0 = not at all, 10 = completely/always. Use the last 4 weeks as the reference period. Keep the pace conversational — invite brief reflection on each score before moving on.

**P — Positive Emotion**
*How often do you experience positive feelings in daily life?*

1. How often do you feel genuinely happy or content? (0–10)
2. How often do you experience joy, pleasure, or delight? (0–10)
3. How often do you feel grateful for your life as it is? (0–10)

P Score: ___ (average of 3 answers)

**E — Engagement**
*How often are you fully absorbed in what you're doing?*

4. How often do you lose track of time because you are so absorbed in something? (0–10)
5. How often do activities feel effortless and natural — in a state of flow? (0–10)
6. How often do you feel fully present and focused in what you do? (0–10)

E Score: ___ (average of 3 answers)

**R — Relationships**
*How connected and supported do you feel by the people in your life?*

7. How loved and cared for do you feel by the people close to you? (0–10)
8. How satisfied are you with the depth and quality of your relationships? (0–10)
9. How often do you receive meaningful support when you need it? (0–10)

R Score: ___ (average of 3 answers)

**M — Meaning**
*Do you have a sense of purpose — that what you do matters?*

10. How clearly can you articulate what your life is for — what you are here to do? (0–10)
11. How often does your daily work feel connected to something larger than yourself? (0–10)
12. How strong is your sense that your life has direction and purpose? (0–10)

M Score: ___ (average of 3 answers)

**A — Achievement**
*Are you making meaningful progress and feeling accomplished?*

13. How often do you make progress on goals that matter to you? (0–10)
14. How capable and effective do you feel in the areas that matter most? (0–10)
15. How satisfied are you with what you are achieving in your life right now? (0–10)

A Score: ___ (average of 3 answers)

**Overall PERMA Score:** ___ (average of all 5 pillar scores)

## Interpretation Guide

**By pillar score:**
- 8–10: This pillar is a genuine strength. Protect and leverage it.
- 6–7: Present but underutilized. A small shift here could yield outsized return.
- 4–5: A real gap. This pillar is likely costing the user without their full awareness.
- 0–3: Severely depleted. Needs attention before other pillars can be rebuilt.

**By profile pattern:**
- **High P, low M:** Pleasure without purpose. Life feels good but hollow. Explore values and contribution.
- **High A, low E:** Achieving through willpower, not flow. Burnout risk. Explore what genuinely engages them vs. what they feel they should be doing.
- **High M, low P:** Purpose but no joy — martyrdom or grinding. Explore permission to enjoy the journey.
- **Low R across the board:** Isolation is often a silent root cause for all other low pillars. Address connection first.
- **All pillars low:** Likely burnout or depression. Surface this gently and assess what external support is needed.
- **Inconsistent E:** Flow is highly context-dependent — explore which activities produce it and which kill it.

**PERMA vs. Wheel of Life:**
PERMA measures the quality of the inner experience; Wheel of Life measures satisfaction across life domains. A person can score high on Career in the Wheel and low on E and M in PERMA — they're achieving externally but not engaged or finding meaning. Both views together are more powerful than either alone.

## Follow-Up Questions
- Which pillar score surprised you most, high or low?
- If you could strengthen just one pillar, which would have the biggest ripple effect on the others?
- When was the last time you felt genuinely flourishing? Which pillars were high then?
- For your lowest pillar: is this a recent change or has it been low for a long time?
- What's one activity or relationship that reliably moves your [lowest pillar] score up?
- Your [highest pillar] is a real strength — how are you using it? Could you lean on it more?
- What would need to be true for your overall score to be 8+ across all pillars?

## Pitfalls
- Don't treat low Positive Emotion as synonymous with depression — it's a spectrum; stay curious, not clinical
- Achievement is not about external success — redirect if user starts listing credentials; ask about felt progress
- Meaning is often underdeveloped in high-achievers — don't skip it because someone looks successful
- Relationships score can be artificially inflated by quantity (lots of colleagues) rather than quality (real connection) — probe the depth
- Engagement (flow) requires both skill and challenge to coexist — if someone scores low, explore whether they're bored (too easy) or anxious (too hard)
- PERMA is descriptive, not prescriptive — resist the urge to solve every low score in the same session
