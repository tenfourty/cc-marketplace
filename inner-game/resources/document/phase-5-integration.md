## Overview

Integration is the fifth and final phase of creating The Document — and the only phase with no end. Having crafted the declarations, the work now is to live from them. The Document is not a document you complete; it is a practice you maintain. Read aloud morning and evening, used as a lens for decisions, and periodically reviewed for alignment. The goal of integration is not perfection — no one lives their declarations perfectly. The goal is awareness: noticing when you are living from your Document and when you are not, and using that noticing to return.

## When to Use

- User has a completed Document from Phase 4 and is asking "now what?"
- User has been reading their Document but reports it "not working"
- User is using The Document inconsistently — needs support establishing the practice
- User is struggling to connect their Document to real decisions and behavior
- Low alignment scores are persisting — may need guidance before a revision is triggered

## When NOT to Use

- User hasn't finished Phase 4 — integration cannot begin without a complete Document
- User wants to revise The Document — use revision-guide.md instead
- User is in acute crisis — stabilize first

## The Daily Practice

The daily reading practice is not optional. It is the mechanism by which declarations move from words on a page to identity in action. Skipping it is analogous to deciding not to train: the capability does not maintain itself.

### Morning Reading

Read The Document aloud, slowly, with full presence — not quickly as a productivity ritual, but as a deliberate act of self-declaration. The word "aloud" is essential. Silent reading is processed differently by the mind. Hearing your own voice make these declarations activates something different than reading with your eyes.

Guidance for the morning reading:
- Find a moment of stillness before reading — do not read while multitasking
- Read slowly enough that each declaration lands before you move to the next
- If a declaration feels particularly alive, pause and let it be present before continuing
- If a declaration feels hollow or unfamiliar this morning, note it — this is data, not failure
- The reading takes 2-5 minutes for most Documents. Do not rush it.

### Evening Alignment Check

In the evening — before sleep, not during the day's activity — take a few minutes to review how the day went against The Document.

The question is not "Did I perform my declarations?" It is: "Which declarations felt alive in me today? Which ones felt absent?"

A simple scoring approach: For each declaration, 1-10. Where were you living from this today? Where were you absent from it?

This is not a self-criticism exercise. It is an observation exercise. A low score on a declaration means: this one needs attention. Not: you have failed.

Keep the scoring honest. A consistent pattern of low scores on a specific declaration is information: either this area of your life needs more deliberate attention, or this declaration needs revision (see revision-guide.md).

### The Document as Decision Lens

As integration deepens, The Document begins to function as a decision-making filter. Before significant decisions, the question becomes: "What does the person I have declared myself to be do here?"

This is not asking what you want to do, or what would be easiest, or what would make you look good. It is asking: what does my declared identity demand in this situation?

Examples:
- A person who has declared "I am someone who tells the truth even when it is costly" faces a moment where a comfortable silence would serve their short-term interests. The Document provides the answer before the calculation begins.
- A person who has declared "I am someone who acts from courage, especially when I am afraid" notices fear arising before a difficult conversation. The declaration does not eliminate the fear — it tells them what to do with it.

The Document does not make decisions for you. It creates a context in which you make them.

## The Integration Rhythm

Integration is not an event — it is a practice. The rhythm:

**Daily:** Morning reading (aloud), evening alignment check (1-10 per declaration, note patterns)

**Weekly:** Brief review of the week's alignment scores. Where are the persistent lows? What was happening when those declarations felt most alive? Most absent?

**Monthly:** Slower read of The Document. Does it still feel like you? Are there declarations that have settled in completely and feel entirely natural? Are there declarations where you are still regularly failing to live them? (Note: this does not necessarily mean revision — it may mean continued focus.)

**At major transitions or after 6-12 months:** Full alignment review — see revision-guide.md for triggers and process.

## Working with Low Alignment

Low alignment on a declaration on any given day is not a problem — it is the practice working exactly as it should. The alignment check is not a grade; it is a compass reading.

However, patterns matter. Guide the user to distinguish:

**Situational low alignment** — a declaration scored low today because of specific circumstances (high stress, external pressure, unusual context). Not a concern. Note it, return to the declaration tomorrow.

**Domain challenge** — a declaration consistently scores lower than others because this particular area of life requires more attention and practice. Healthy. This is the declaration doing its job — it is naming where the growth edge currently is.

**Persistent low alignment** — a declaration consistently scores 5 or below over 2+ weeks, and the user cannot identify a clear reason tied to external circumstances. This is a revision trigger. The declaration may need to be revisited: is it still true? Is it too aspirational? Has something changed? See revision-guide.md.

**The Document feeling irrelevant** — the user stops thinking about The Document during the day; it has become a morning recitation that feels disconnected from life. This is a sign that integration hasn't fully happened yet. Return to using it explicitly as a decision lens: "Before I do anything significant today, I will ask: what does my Document say about this?"

## When It Starts Working

Integration is working when:
- The user catches themselves in a moment of misalignment — not after the fact, but in the moment — and makes a different choice
- The user makes a difficult decision and reports "The Document made this clear"
- The morning reading feels like returning home rather than reciting a list
- The declarations no longer feel like something to aspire to but like accurate descriptions of who the user is

These are not destinations — they are occasional experiences that become more frequent over time. Steve Hardison's reported practice of reading his Document daily for decades is a testament to the fact that this practice never becomes unnecessary. The declarations deepen; they do not become obsolete.

## The Long Arc

The Document is not a project with an end date. Hardison's practice spans decades. Most people who do this work find that The Document evolves over years — not because the declarations were wrong, but because people grow and the declarations need to grow with them.

The expectation to set at the beginning of integration:
- Months 1-3: Establishing the daily practice. Getting the morning reading into muscle memory. Learning to use the evening check without it becoming self-punishment.
- Months 3-12: The Document begins to function as a real lens. Specific declarations come alive in specific situations. Some declarations feel fully inhabited; others still represent a growth edge.
- Year 1+: Deep familiarity with the declarations. The ability to hear when a declaration needs revision vs when life circumstances are simply testing it. The beginning of a relationship with The Document as a living document rather than a fixed text.

## Key Questions

- "Which declaration feels most alive in you right now? Which one feels most absent?"
- "When in the past week did you act from your Document without thinking about it? When did you act against it?"
- "If you were truly living this declaration, what would be different about how you are approaching this situation right now?"
- "What does low alignment on this declaration consistently tell you about where you are investing your attention?"
- "Who would you be, in this specific situation, if you were fully living this declaration?"

## Completion Criteria

Integration has no completion criteria — it is an ongoing practice. However, the practice is "established" when:
1. Morning reading is consistent for at least 4 weeks with minimal prompting
2. The user can cite at least one example where The Document influenced a real decision
3. The evening alignment check is honest — not uniformly high (which would indicate performance) and not uniformly low (which would indicate resignation), but variable and informative
4. The user reports The Document as a real presence in their life, not just a document they read

## Pitfalls

**Treating the reading as a productivity ritual.** Reading quickly, checking it off the list. The morning reading must be done with presence. If it is being rushed, slow it down to the point where it feels uncomfortable — that is closer to the right speed.

**Using the alignment check for self-punishment.** A 4/10 on a declaration is not failure. It is information. If the user is approaching the evening check with dread or self-criticism, reframe it explicitly as a compass rather than a grade.

**Abandoning the practice during difficult periods.** When life is hard, the morning reading feels trivial. This is exactly when it is most important. Gently hold the user to the practice: "What would the person in your Document do with this difficulty?"

**Waiting for perfection before "really" starting.** Some users treat the daily practice as provisional — "I'll really start when the Document is finalized." The Document is finalized enough. Start today.

**Keeping a low-alignment declaration too long without revisiting it.** The Document should be a source of grounding, not ongoing guilt. If a declaration has scored below 5 for more than two weeks consistently, take it to the revision process rather than continuing to feel inadequate in front of it.
