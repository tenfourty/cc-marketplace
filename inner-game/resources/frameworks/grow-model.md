## Overview
GROW is the most widely used coaching model: Goal, Reality, Options, Will (or Way Forward). It provides a simple, reliable structure for a coaching conversation that moves from aspiration to committed action. Whitmore designed it as a non-directive framework — the coach asks questions, the coachee does the thinking.

## When to Use
- User presents a goal, decision, or challenge with some clarity about what they want
- Session needs structure — user is scattered or unfocused
- User is stuck between options and needs to think it through systematically
- Goal-setting conversations (OKRs, career moves, project kickoffs)
- Follow-up sessions tracking progress on a prior commitment

## When NOT to Use
- User is in acute distress — emotional processing comes first (use motivational interviewing or active listening)
- The goal itself is uncertain or unclear — use solution-focused miracle question first to surface what they actually want
- User is already deep in action and just needs accountability — skip to Will
- Problem is values-level, not goal-level — use ACT hexaflex or co-active instead

## The Framework

### G — Goal
Establish what the coachee wants to achieve from this session and longer term. Distinguish between end goals (ultimate aspiration) and performance goals (what is within their control). Make goals specific, measurable, and owned by the coachee — not what they think they should want.

### R — Reality
Explore the current situation without judgment. Encourage self-assessment over coach interpretation. Surface assumptions, relevant facts, what has already been tried, and what is actually happening versus what is believed to be happening. This is the hardest stage to do well — coaches rush past it.

### O — Options
Generate a full range of possible options before evaluating any of them. Quantity over quality first. The coach's job is to expand the option space, not propose the answer. Only after options are surfaced does the coachee evaluate and select.

### W — Will (Way Forward)
Convert the chosen option(s) into specific, time-bound actions. Establish commitment level. Surface and address obstacles. End with a concrete next step the coachee has chosen and committed to.

## Key Questions

**Goal**
- "What do you want to achieve?"
- "What would a good outcome for this session look like?"
- "How would you know you had achieved it?"
- "Is this goal within your control?"

**Reality**
- "What is happening now?"
- "How do you know that is accurate?"
- "What have you already tried?"
- "What is the impact of this situation on you — and on others?"
- "What is stopping you from moving forward?"

**Options**
- "What could you do? What else?"
- "If you had more time / resources / confidence, what would you do?"
- "What would you advise a friend in this situation?"
- "What is the wildest option you haven't considered?"
- "What are the pros and cons of each option?"

**Will**
- "Which option will you act on?"
- "What will you do, and by when?"
- "On a scale of 1–10, how committed are you to doing this?"
- "If it's not a 10, what would make it a 10?"
- "What obstacles might arise, and how will you handle them?"

## Pitfalls
- **Rushing Reality**: Coaches move to Options before the current situation is fully understood. Reality is where insight lives — slow down here.
- **Offering options**: The moment the coach suggests an option, it becomes the most likely one chosen. Hold back.
- **Weak Will stage**: "I'll try" is not a commitment. Push for specific action, specific time, and a real commitment score.
- **Treating GROW as linear**: Real sessions loop — a new Goal may emerge mid-Reality, an Option may reveal a different real goal. Stay flexible.
- **Ignoring the inner game**: GROW addresses outer structure but not inner interference (self-doubt, limiting beliefs). Pair with inner-game or CBT coaching when blocks appear.
