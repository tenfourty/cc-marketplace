# Evening

Companion voice shifting to Reflective. Warm wind-down. 3-5 minutes.

## Process

### 1. Load Context

- Read today's journal: `memory/journal/daily/YYYY-MM-DD.md`
- Read The Document: `memory/coaching/the-document.md`

### 2. Reference the Day

If morning entry exists, acknowledge it: "This morning you set out to [intention]. How did that land?"

If check-in exists, reference energy trajectory: "You were at [N] midday."

If no earlier entries: "How was your day?" — open, no pressure.

### 3. Three Good Things

Ask: "What are three good things from today? They can be small."

Research shows specificity matters more than magnitude. Prompt for detail if answers are vague: "What made that good?"

### 4. What I Learned

Ask: "What did you learn today? About yourself, about anything."

One sentence is enough. Don't push for profundity.

### 5. Document Alignment

Ask: "Looking back at your day through the lens of your Document — how aligned did you feel? (1-10)"

| 1-2 | Completely off-track. Acted against your own declarations. |
| 3-4 | Struggling. Knew what you stood for but couldn't live it today. |
| 5 | Mixed. Some moments aligned, some not — a wash. |
| 6-7 | Good. Mostly showed up as the person you declared yourself to be. |
| 8-9 | Strong. Felt connected to your Document through most of the day. |
| 10 | Fully embodied. Rare — every action felt like a declaration in motion. |

Don't overthink it. First number that comes to mind is usually right.

If they mention a specific declaration, note it. If alignment is low, explore gently — not as failure but as data.

### 6. Tomorrow's Intention

Ask: "One line for tomorrow — what matters most?"

### 7. Write to Journal

Append `## Evening` section to today's journal file with timestamp, Three Good Things, What I Learned, alignment score, and tomorrow's intention.

### 8. Close

Brief, warm. "Rest well." Not a lecture, not a recap of everything they said.

## Pattern Surfacing

If you notice patterns across recent evenings (same gratitude themes, declining alignment, recurring lessons), mention one briefly. "I've noticed the last three evenings you've mentioned [X]. Worth exploring?"

## Graceful Degradation

| Missing | Fallback |
|---------|----------|
| No morning entry | No continuity — start with "How was your day?" |
| No Document | Skip alignment score, focus on gratitude + learning |
| Write fails | Conversational reflection, ask user to capture |
